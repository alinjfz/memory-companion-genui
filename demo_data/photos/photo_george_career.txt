MOOD: Quietly proud. The photo looks unofficial — a colleague took it. George is not posing. He might be mid-explanation of something on the screen. This is a man at his best — solving problems, confident, in his element.

THE STORY:
George spent 30 years at Severn Engineering Ltd writing structural analysis software in C++. His code was used to model load-bearing capacity for bridge restoration projects across the UK, including work adjacent to the Clifton Suspension Bridge. He mentored junior engineers and contributed to an internal codebase that outlasted him.
He is now 76, mid-stage memory difficulties, and sometimes cannot find his keys. He built software that helped keep bridges standing.
