--- WHAT THE PHOTO LOOKS LIKE ---

THIS IS A SIDE-BY-SIDE DIPTYCH (two photos combined into one image, split vertically down the middle with a thin white line).

LEFT PANEL — Bristol Royal Infirmary, 1985:
- Young George, 35 years old. Dark brown hair, strong jaw. Wearing a pale blue short-sleeved shirt. Standing in a hospital corridor, cream-painted walls behind him.
- He is cradling baby Helen — days old, wrapped in a blue NHS blanket. He is looking down at her, not at the camera. His face is the face of someone who has just realised life will never be the same.
- Lighting: Slightly washed-out hospital fluorescent light, soft around the edges. This looks like a photo taken on a 1985 35mm camera — slightly warm, slightly grainy.

RIGHT PANEL — Royal Mile, Edinburgh, 2024:
- George, now 74 years old. Silver-haired, slightly stooped, wearing a grey overcoat and a red scarf. Standing on a cobbled street with Edinburgh's Old Town visible behind.
- Helen, 39 years old. Dark hair (she looks like George — same jaw, same eyes). She has her arm firmly looped through his, her shoulder close to his. She is turned slightly toward him, not the camera, attentive.
- George is smiling at something out of frame. He looks warm but frail. She looks steady.
- Lighting: Cool, bright autumn Edinburgh light. Feels like October, early afternoon.

CAPTION (optional, for print or presentation): No caption needed. The image says everything.

ASSEMBLY NOTE: If you can't find a perfect diptych match, two separate photos printed side by side work equally well. The key emotional beat: LEFT = he holds her. RIGHT = she holds him. Mirror image of love.
